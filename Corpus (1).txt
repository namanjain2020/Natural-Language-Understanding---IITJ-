Academic programmes at Indian Institute of Technology, Jodhpur are designed to develop the highest calibre human resource capable of understanding the new patterns of knowledge creation across disciplines obliterating traditional boundaries between science, humanities, social sciences and engineering. IIT Jodhpur aims to produce quality professionals who would be able to address profound and wide-ranging societal challenges of the twenty-first century such as energy, food, water, housing, mobility, and health. In addition to imparting scientific knowledge, IIT Jodhpur endeavours to inculcate human qualities of courage, integrity, fairness, humility and team effort among its graduates through curricular, co-curricular and extra-curricular activities on campus. The undergraduate academic programmes focus on developing a temper for the life long process of learning, creative thinking and exploring. The postgraduate academic programmes focus on developing a deep understanding of the subject of study coupled with creative inquisitiveness and the ability to address and solve new problems with a free and objective Mind. The academic programmes are based on three tenets of semester system, Credit System and Relative Grading. These academic programmes are administered by a duly constituted Academic Committee through the office of Academics. The Academic Committee will be chaired by Dean Academics. In the absence of Dean Academics, Associate Dean Academics will discharge the responsibilities of Dean Academics. The Senate of the Institute has formulated a set of guidelines and rules to ensure a high standard of performance as well as for smooth functioning of the academic programmes. Within this broad set of guidelines, subject to the approval of Senate, different programmes can include additional academic requirements as deemed necessary for that programme. These academic programmes are continuously monitored by the Senate and make appropriate modifications or improvements as and when necessary. The academic session normally runs from the end of July in one year to the middle of July in the next year. The academic session is divided into three parts including two regular semesters and a summer term as per the following timelines. Semester I runs from the fourth week of July to the last week of November. Semester II runs from the last week of December to the last week of April. Summer Term runs from the middle of May to the middle of July. Excluding the days of the examinations, the total number of days of instruction in a semester is at least seventy. The academic calendar gives the exact dates of all important events during the Academic Session, such as orientation, registration, the commencement of classes, adding and dropping of courses, submission of documents, examinations, submission of grades, project evaluation, declaration of results, mid-semester recess, and vacation. This calendar is approved by the Senate. Students are required to register for prescribed credits as per the programme, on the dates specified in the Academic Calendar. If the student does not register by the date of registration, she or he can register by paying the fine and register before the date of late registration. Provisional Registration allows a fresh student joining the Institute, who is awaiting the final results of the qualifying examination, to register provisionally on submission of a certificate from her or his last Institute stating that she or he has appeared in the final examination of the qualifying degree. For Late Registration, if the student does not register by the date of late registration, he or she will be deregistered. The appeal against deregistration may be made to Chairman, Senate through Dean Academics. Adding and Dropping of Courses means a student may add or drop courses with approval of the Faculty advisor till the last date of registration. Withdrawing from a Course means after the last date of add-drop, a student will have the option of withdrawing from a course, usually within four weeks from the beginning of the Semester. The withdrawn course will be mentioned in the Student’s grade card with a letter grade W. Cancellation of Semester Registration occurs for absence for a period of four or more weeks during a semester which shall result in automatic cancellation of the registration of a student from all the courses in that semester. Summer Course Registration means a department may offer summer courses to enable the students to clear their backlog courses or regular credit courses, provided a faculty member is available.
The medium of instruction is English. Course Structure dictates all courses have associated credits based on contact hours including Lectures, Tutorials, and Practicals. Courses are assigned levels based on academic maturity. Approval of Courses means the Senate approves each course along with its weight in terms of credits. List of Courses is finalized before the semester by the hosting Department. Categories of Courses include Regular Course with fourteen hours of classroom engagement per one credit, Self Study Course for elective or core courses not running in that semester with proper approvals, and Independent Study Course with a maximum of three credits enabling a student to pursue an academic topic of interest under a faculty member's supervision requiring a cumulative grade point average greater than or equal to six. Conduct of Courses dictates courses can be conducted via direct contact, synchronized remote contact, or guided study mode. All formal evaluations must happen under direct physical supervision. Auditing of Courses is allowed in addition to prescribed academic load with permission of the Instructor and Faculty Advisor. Attendance Requirements state a student should have full attendance. If attendance falls below seventy-five percent, they must score at least a C grade to pass the course otherwise they are awarded an F. For Evaluation, performance is evaluated continuously via classroom interaction, quizzes, assignments, tutorials, lab work, term papers, and projects. Minor examinations and Major examinations are mandatory for a regular fourteen-week lecture course with a weightage of forty to sixty percent.Relative letter grades are awarded on a ten-point scale. A star grade is ten points for exceptional performance only for courses with more than fifty students and maximum two percent of class. A grade is ten points for outstanding performance. A minus grade is nine points for excellent performance. B grade is eight points for very good performance. B minus grade is seven points for good performance. C grade is six points for average performance. C minus grade is five points for below average performance. D grade is four points for marginal performance. E grade is two points for poor performance and eligible for one additional examination to upgrade to D. F grade is zero points for fail. Special Grades include I for Incomplete, S for Satisfactory, X for Thesis Continuation, U for Unsatisfactory, W for Withdrawn. Procedure for declaration of results includes evaluations finalized by instructors, moderated by committees, recommended by the Head of Department, approved by the Dean, and finalized by the Chairman Senate. Results once approved cannot be changed except via formal appeal to the Chairman, Senate. Semester Grade Point Average is a weighted average of grades in a specific semester. Cumulative Grade Point Average is overall academic performance in all registered courses up to the latest completed semester. Grade Card and Transcript are issued at the end of each semester or programme, indicating courses, credits, and grades completed at IIT Jodhpur. Waiver of Requirements in Special Cases means rules may be waived in special circumstances by the Academic Committee on the recommendation of the Department with the approval of Chairman, Senate. Make-up Examinations are permitted for missed minor or major exams due to genuine medical reasons requiring Medical Officer endorsement. They are held during the makeup or supplementary week. Prorating is strictly prohibited. IIT Jodhpur offers Bachelor of Technology programs, and capability-linked opportunities like Minor or Interdisciplinary Specializations, Department Area Specializations, and Dual Degrees. Undergraduate Admissions are handled via Joint Entrance Examination Advanced. Foreign nationals are admitted via Indian Council of Cultural Relations Cultural Exchange. Student Exchange Programme permits reciprocal one or two-semester visits. Credit transfer allowed up to twenty credits per semester. Visiting Students can complete part of their requirements at IIT Jodhpur via sponsored application. Curriculum and Academic Requirements include Capability Linked Opportunities. Eligibility means a student must earn a minimum one hundred credits with cumulative grade point average greater than or equal to six at the end of sixth semester. For Dual Degree, minimum one hundred thirty-four credits with cumulative grade point average greater than or equal to six at the end of seventh semester. Requirements include twenty extra credits for Minor or Specialization, and fifty-six extra credits for Dual Degree. Maximum Duration is fourteen registered semesters for Bachelor of Technology. Academic Performance Requirements for continuation after the second semester dictate a student must earn a minimum of eighteen credits or fourteen for specific reserved categories. Failing this allows for one restart. Assistantship for Dual-degree is eligible if they have earned one hundred fifty credits by the eighth semester and have either qualified GATE or have a cumulative grade point average greater than eight. Summer Courses allow maximum eight credits primarily for academically deficient students to clear backlogs. Limits are minimum twelve credits and maximum twenty-five credits per semester. Exceptions include under-load with maximum sixteen credits for students on probation. Over-load allowed for non-probation students with cumulative grade point average greater than or equal to seven point five. Evaluation of Special undergraduate only courses includes specific evaluation procedures for Bachelor Projects, Engineering Innovation, Design Projects, and Entrepreneurship Projects evaluated by Department Undergraduate Committee via report and viva-voce. Termination means a student may be terminated for unauthorized absence for eight or more weeks, failing to register, disciplinary violations like ragging, failing to earn minimum credits within maximum duration, or academic probation for two consecutive semesters. Appeals can be made to the Chairman, Senate. Leave Rules include Short Leave with maximum ten days for medical and five days for other valid reasons. Semester Withdrawal happens for absence for four or more weeks resulting in automatic withdrawal. Academic Leave has a maximum one summer term and two semesters allowed for fieldwork, exchange programs, internships. Branch Change is permitted after completion of the first year. Eligibility requires completed all prescribed credits, no backlogs, no disciplinary action. Cumulative grade point average criteria is greater than or equal to eight point five for General or OBC, and greater than or equal to seven point five for reserved categories. To move to Engineering Science, the criteria is greater than or equal to eight, or seven for reserved categories. Branch Change will be discontinued from Academic Year starting twenty twenty-six. Requirements for Graduation include Residence with minimum six semesters for Bachelor of Technology and eight for Dual Degree. Academic means fulfill credit requirements. Dues means no Dues Clearance required. Discipline means no pending disciplinary actions. Extra-curricular means completion of specific national service or sports activities for at least two semesters. Postgraduate programs offered include Master of Science, Master of Science and Master of Technology Dual degree, Master of Technology, Master of Technology and Doctor of Philosophy Dual degree, and Doctor of Philosophy. Postgraduate Admissions Categories include Full-time regular, Full-time sponsored, Part-time, External, Executive. Eligibility varies by program but generally requires sixty percent marks or fifty-five percent for reserved categories, or six point zero cumulative grade point average or five point five for reserved categories in qualifying degree. Valid national examination scores are often required for regular technical or science admissions. Postgraduate Registration Limits for Master of Science are ten to twenty-four credits. Master of Technology and Doctor of Philosophy limits vary heavily by category. Registration Kept Alive means Master of Technology or Doctor of Philosophy students can maintain student status for a maximum of six semesters while pursuing professional activities. Maximum Duration is eight semesters for Master of Science, twelve semesters including registration kept alive for Master of Technology, and fourteen semesters including registration kept alive for Doctor of Philosophy or Dual degree. Academic Requirements dictate Credit Requirements are dictated by the specific discipline ranging from sixty to seventy-seven credits for Masters programs. Doctor of Philosophy coursework requires twelve to thirty credits depending on the qualifying degree, plus forty-eight thesis credits. Academic Performance for Continuation and Graduation requires minimum cumulative grade point average of five point zero for Masters, seven point zero for Doctor of Philosophy continuation, and seven point five for Doctor of Philosophy graduation. Fellowship Continuation requires minimum cumulative grade point average of six point five for Master of Technology and seven point zero for Doctor of Philosophy. Leave Rules dictate maximum thirty days in an academic year. Doctor of Philosophy maximum fifteen days at a stretch. Masters maximum ten days. Maternity leave sanctioned per national directives. Academic leave permitted up to one year for collaborative work or fieldwork. Financial Assistance is available to eligible full-time regular students via national or project funds requiring valid qualification scores where applicable. Doctor of Philosophy enhanced scholarship available after two years upon completing qualifier requirements. Rules and Regulations for Doctor of Philosophy or Dual Degree dictate Supervisor is allotted by the end of the first semester. Annual Progress Monitoring is evaluated yearly via report and poster presentation. Two consecutive Unsatisfactory grades lead to termination. Qualifier Requirements dictate students must complete coursework, comprehensive exam, State of Art Seminar, and Research Proposal defense usually before the fifth semester. Synopsis and Thesis must be submitted within specific timelines, evaluated by internal and external examiners including at least one from abroad and one from India, followed by a public Viva Voce defense. Graduation Requirements for postgraduate dictate Residence with minimum four semesters for Master of Science, three for Master of Technology, two to five for Doctor of Philosophy depending on entry qualifications. It also requires fulfillment of credits, minimum cumulative grade point average, no pending dues, and no disciplinary actions. The Senate of IIT Jodhpur reserves the right to modify or amend these rules without notice. The Master of Technology program in Computer Science and Engineering at IIT Jodhpur establishes a balance between traditional and emerging areas of the discipline by offering students a combined knowledge of theory and practice. The program is designed to extend the undergraduate computing skills with up-to-date and in-depth expertise in specialized areas of the computer science discipline. The curriculum is developed in consultation with experts from academia and industry. Students are equipped with relevant concepts, algorithms, techniques, and underpinning principles while developing the ability to solve real-world problems in a number of application areas such as Computer Systems and Security, Distributed Computing, Internet of Things, Artificial Intelligence, and Big Data. The Master of Technology in Artificial Intelligence aims to fill the critical needs of the industry and to fill the gap in the availability of highly skilled AI scientists and engineers. The major goal of the two-year program is to impart rigorous training in strong foundations of artificial intelligence as well as cutting-edge AI tools and techniques, and produce graduates who can reinforce the country's efforts towards developing AI technologies. The Dual Masters Program in Computer Science and Cybersecurity in collaboration with University at Albany leverages the strengths of the two institutions. The field of Cybersecurity is becoming more critical as we become more technology dependent. IIT Jodhpur has been offering dual-degree programs since July twenty nineteen designed particularly for students with an inclination for research. The students can also work on industry projects and collaborate with faculty from other academic and research bodies. This program offers the benefit of doctoral level scholarship from the first year itself and the integrated courses and research helps in reducing the overall time of completing the degrees. The Executive program for working professionals runs as a combination of synchronous and asynchronous teaching where online lectures are offered on evenings or weekends for the convenience of working professionals.
